# Understanding Your Vitals

We often only think about our vital signs when we're at the doctor's office. But what if tracking these metrics daily could provide a window into your long-term health?

## What are "Vitals"?

Vital signs are measurements of the body's most basic functions. The four main vital signs routinely monitored by medical professionals include:

1.  **Body temperature**
2.  **Pulse rate (Heart rate)**
3.  **Respiration rate**
4.  **Blood pressure** (Though not strictly a vital sign, it's often measured along with them)

## The Power of the Baseline

The real value of tracking vitals isn't in a single measurement, but in understanding your **baseline**. Everyone's body is different. What's "normal" for one person might be slightly high or low for another.

By tracking your vitals over time with OrionHealth, you can:

-   **Identify Trends:** Notice if your resting heart rate is gradually increasing, which could indicate stress or overtraining.
-   **Detect Early Warnings:** A sudden change in your baseline can be an early indicator of illness, sometimes before you even feel symptoms.
-   **Have Better Conversations with your Doctor:** Instead of saying "I've been feeling tired," you can show your doctor data: "My resting heart rate has been 10 beats higher than usual for the last two weeks."

## Small Habits, Big Impact

You don't need expensive equipment to start. Many smartwatches track heart rate automatically, and simple tools like a thermometer or blood pressure cuff are affordable additions to any home.

Taking two minutes a day to check in with your body can lead to a lifetime of better health.
